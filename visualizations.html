Average Laptop Prices by Brand

This visualization shows average laptop prices for various brands. Razer, Huawei, and Apple have the highest average prices, indicating their premium positioning. mid-range brands like ASUS, Dell, and HP offer a balance of price and performance. Budget brands, such as RDP and Micromax, have the lowest average prices, targeting cost-conscious buyers. This visualization highlights the pricing hierarchy and market segmentation among laptop brands.

Distribution of Laptop Prices

This visualization shows the distribution of laptop prices in the dataset. Most laptops are priced below 100,000(INR), indicating a focus on affordability. The frequency of laptops decreases as prices increase beyond 100,000(INR). The distribution is positively skewed, with a few high-priced laptops. This suggests that the market primarily targets budget and mid-range consumers.

Interactive Price vs Battery Life Analysis

This visualization shows the relationship between laptop price and battery life. Each dot represents a laptop, with its color indicating the brand. By hovering over a dot, you can see detailed information like price, battery life, and specifications. The chart helps identify patterns, such as higher-priced laptops often having longer battery life, though there are exceptions. It also highlights which brands dominate certain price ranges, making it easier to compare options and find laptops that offer the best value for their price.

RAM vs Price Analysis (D3)

This visualization shows the relationship between laptop price and RAM size. Each dot represents a laptop, with its color indicating the brand. By hovering over a dot, you can see detailed information like price, RAM, and processor type. Laptops with higher RAM generally have higher prices, but some brands offer affordable options with higher RAM. The chart also highlights premium models with significantly higher prices, helping to identify both budget-friendly and high-end options.

Feature Correlation with Price

This visualization shows how different laptop features are correlated with price. RAM has the strongest positive correlation, meaning laptops with more RAM are generally more expensive. Features like adapter power and display quality also significantly impact the price. SSD size and processor type moderately affect pricing, while laptops with HDDs tend to be less expensive. Overall, the chart highlights that hardware specifications are key factors in determining laptop prices.
